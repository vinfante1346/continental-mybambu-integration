# Health Services Revenue Model - Differentiated by Customer Segment

**600,000 Customers | 112,000 MAU | Segment-Specific Pricing & Net Revenue**

---

## üìä Executive Summary

### Why Different Net Revenue by Segment?

Each customer segment has different characteristics that affect their value:

**Always Active Customers**:
- Already engaged and paying
- Higher service utilization
- Premium willingness-to-pay
- Lower incentives needed
- **Higher net revenue potential**

**Dormant Customers**:
- Need reactivation incentives
- Moderate service utilization
- Price-sensitive (why they left)
- Require discounts to return
- **Medium net revenue potential**

**Inactive Customers**:
- Never transacted = lowest intent
- Lowest service utilization expected
- Highest incentives needed
- Steeper discounts required
- **Lower net revenue potential**

## üí∞ Differentiated Net Revenue Assumptions

### Segment-Specific Economics

| Segment | Gross Price | Incentives/Discounts | COGS | **Net Revenue** | Rationale |
|---------|-------------|---------------------|------|----------------|-----------|
| **Always Active** | $25/month | -$2 (10% discount) | -$19 | **$4.00/month** | Premium users, high usage, full-price |
| **Dormant** | $20/month | -$5 (25% discount) | -$13 | **$2.50/month** | Need incentives, medium usage, discounted |
| **Inactive** | $15/month | -$7 (50% discount) | -$10 | **$1.50/month** | Steep discounts, low usage, intro pricing |

### Why These Numbers Make Sense

**Always Active ($4 net)**:
- Gross price: $25/month (telehealth + prescriptions + wellness)
- Small discount: $2 (existing customer loyalty)
- COGS: $19 (higher usage = higher costs but economies of scale)
- **Net: $4.00** (16% margin on gross)
- These users maximize value and need minimal incentives

**Dormant ($2.50 net)**:
- Gross price: $20/month (core telehealth + prescriptions)
- Medium discount: $5 (win-back offer "25% off to return")
- COGS: $13 (moderate usage expected)
- **Net: $2.50** (12.5% margin on gross)
- Need incentives but will use service moderately

**Inactive ($1.50 net)**:
- Gross price: $15/month (basic telehealth only)
- Heavy discount: $7 ("50% off for 6 months to try us")
- COGS: $10 (low usage expected, cherry-pick benefits)
- **Net: $1.50** (10% margin on gross)
- Breaking even almost, but gets them in the door

## üí° Strategic Insights by Segment

### Insight 1: Always Active Drives 55% of Revenue with 39% of Users

**The Math**:
- 7,263 users @ $4/mo = $29,052 MRR (55% of total)
- 8,079 users @ $2.50/mo = $20,198 MRR (38% of total)
- 3,150 users @ $1.50/mo = $4,725 MRR (7% of total)

**Implication**: Focus on Always Active for maximum revenue impact

**Action**: Allocate 50% of budget to Always Active (up from 40%)

---

### Insight 2: Dormant Provides Volume but at Lower ARPU

**The Math**:
- 268k available vs 112k Always Active (2.4x larger)
- 5% conversion (vs 10% Always) = 13,400 users
- But $2.50 net (vs $4) = 37.5% lower revenue per user

**Implication**: Need volume to compensate for lower ARPU

**Action**: Aggressive reactivation campaigns justified by scale

---

### Insight 3: Inactive Economics Are Marginal

**The Math**:
- $1.50 net revenue √ó 14.3 months = $21 LTV
- $12 CAC = 1.75:1 ratio (vs 3:1 industry standard)
- 220k available but 2.5% conversion = only 5,500 users

**Implication**: Barely profitable, high risk

**Action**:
- Test with small budget (10-15%)
- If LTV doesn't improve to $25+, cut spending
- Focus on card activation (might improve economics)
